Poster Presentation Instructions

To display your poster at the conference, please adhere to the following specifications:

  • Size: A0 (84.1 cm × 118.9 cm / 33.11 in × 46.81 in)
  • Orientation: Portrait

If your poster corresponds to an accepted paper that is being presented in the main conference program, please use the following template which indicates your paper presentation session.

Instructions to Authors

Poster-only submissions consist of two pages of an extended abstract (PDF format) describing your work.

  • Submit abstracts of no more than two single-spaced pages, including figures, tables, and references.
  • Use A4 or US Letter paper size, with all text and figures fitting inside a 178 × 229 mm (7 × 9 in) block centered on the page, using two columns separated by ≥8 mm (0.33″) whitespace.
  • Use ≥10-point font (e.g., Times Roman, Linux Libertine) on ≥12-point leading (single-spaced), including figure and table captions.
  • Most of these rules are automatically applied when using the official SIGPLAN LaTeX or MS Word templates (available here).
  • For any paper parameters not defined above, assume those used in the official templates.

We recommend using the following header if you use LaTeX:

\documentclass[sigplan,twocolumn,review]{acmart}
\renewcommand\footnotetextcopyrightpermission[1]{}
\settopmatter{printfolios=true,printacmref=false}
...

Submission Link: https://eurosys26-poster.hotcrp.com/

  • Submissions must include a title, author names, and affiliations.
  • The extended abstracts will be published on the EuroSys 2026 website at the time of the conference, unless authors request otherwise.

All poster submissions will be reviewed by the Poster Program Committee.

Accepted posters must be presented by at least one author during the EuroSys 2026 Poster Session.

There will be a Best Poster Award.

Please address any questions to poster-chairs-2026@eurosys.org
